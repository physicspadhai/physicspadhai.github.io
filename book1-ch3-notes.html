Chapter 3: Current Electricity 🔌

1. Electric Current

Rate of flow of charge through any cross-section of conductor.

$$I = \frac{q}{t} = \frac{ne}{t}$$ SI Unit: Ampere (A) = C/s. Scalar quantity but has direction.

Current Density:

$$\vec{J} = \frac{I}{A} \hat{n} = n e \vec{v}_d$$ Unit: A/m². Vector quantity. Direction along $\vec{v}_d$
🔥 Board Point: Conventional current direction is opposite to electron flow. Current due to +ve and -ve charges both adds up.

2. Drift Velocity

Average velocity with which free electrons get drifted towards +ve end under electric field.

$$\vec{v}_d = -\frac{e\vec{E}}{m}\tau = \frac{I}{n e A}$$ Where $\tau$ = relaxation time ≈ $10^{-14}$ s

Mobility:

$$\mu = \frac{|\vec{v}_d|}{E} = \frac{e\tau}{m}$$ Unit: m²/V-s

3. Ohm's Law

At constant temperature, current through conductor is directly proportional to potential difference.

$$V = IR$$ R = Resistance. Unit: Ohm (Ω)

Vector Form:

$$\vec{J} = \sigma \vec{E}$$ Where $\sigma$ = conductivity = $\frac{1}{\rho}$

Resistivity:

$$R = \rho \frac{l}{A}$$ $\rho$ = resistivity. Unit: Ω-m. Depends on material & temperature only.

PYQ 2023:

Q. Define resistivity. Write its SI unit. How does it vary with temperature for conductor?
Ans: $\rho = RA/l$. Unit Ω-m. For conductor: $\rho = \rho_0[1 + \alpha(T-T_0)]$, increases with temp.

4. Temperature Dependence

For Conductors:

$$R_T = R_0[1 + \alpha(T-T_0)]$$ $\alpha$ = temp coefficient. +ve for metals.

For Semiconductors:

Resistance decreases with temperature. $\alpha$ is negative.

5. Combination of Resistors

Series:

$$R_s = R_1 + R_2 + R_3$$ Same current, voltage divides: $V = V_1 + V_2 + V_3$

Parallel:

$$\frac{1}{R_p} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2} + \frac{1}{R_3}$$ Same voltage, current divides: $I = I_1 + I_2 + I_3$

6. EMF and Internal Resistance

EMF = Work done per unit charge by cell. Terminal voltage $V = E - Ir$

$$I = \frac{E}{R + r}$$ r = internal resistance

7. Kirchhoff's Laws

1. Junction Law:

Sum of currents entering = Sum of currents leaving. $\sum I = 0$. Charge conservation.

2. Loop Law:

Algebraic sum of potential drops in closed loop = 0. $\sum V = 0$. Energy conservation.

PYQ 2024:

Q. State Kirchhoff's laws. Use them to find current in 2Ω resistor.
Ans: Apply junction + loop law. Solve equations for I.

8. Wheatstone Bridge

Balanced when: $\frac{P}{Q} = \frac{R}{S}$

At balance: $I_g = 0$, No current through galvanometer

9. Meter Bridge

Based on Wheatstone bridge principle.

$$\frac{P}{Q} = \frac{R}{S} \Rightarrow \frac{R}{S} = \frac{l}{100-l}$$ Used to find unknown resistance.

10. Potentiometer

Measures EMF without drawing current. More accurate than voltmeter.

Principle:

$$V \propto l \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{E_1}{E_2} = \frac{l_1}{l_2}$$

Applications:

  • Compare EMF of two cells
  • Find internal resistance: $r = R\left(\frac{l_1 - l_2}{l_2}\right)$
Device Use Formula
Wheatstone Bridge Unknown Resistance $\frac{P}{Q} = \frac{R}{S}$
Meter Bridge Unknown Resistance $S = R\frac{(100-l)}{l}$
Potentiometer Compare EMF, Internal r $\frac{E_1}{E_2} = \frac{l_1}{l_2}$
