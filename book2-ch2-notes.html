1. Photoelectric Effect

Emission of electrons when light of suitable frequency falls on metal.

Einstein's Equation:

$h\nu = \phi_0 + K_{max}$
$h\nu = h\nu_0 + eV_0$

$\phi_0$ = Work function, $\nu_0$ = Threshold frequency, $V_0$ = Stopping potential

Important Graphs:

  • $K_{max}$ vs $\nu$ = Straight line, slope = h
  • Photoelectric current vs Intensity = Straight line
  • Current vs Anode potential = Saturates

2. Particle Nature of Light

Photon energy:

$E = h\nu = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$
Momentum: $p = \frac{h}{\lambda} = \frac{E}{c}$

Rest mass of photon = 0

3. de Broglie Hypothesis

Matter has dual nature. Wavelength associated with particle:

$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$

For electron accelerated by V volt:

$\lambda = \frac{12.27}{\sqrt{V}}$ Å

4. Davisson-Germer Experiment

Proved wave nature of electrons by electron diffraction.

Ni crystal used. Maxima at 54V when $\theta = 50°$

$d\sin\theta = n\lambda$
Experimental $\lambda$ matched de Broglie $\lambda$

Previous Year Questions 2026

CBSE 2024:

Q. Work function of metal is 2.14 eV. Light of 300 nm falls on it. Find max KE of emitted electrons.

Ans: $E = \frac{1240}{300} = 4.13$ eV. $KE_{max} = 4.13 - 2.14 = 1.99$ eV

CBSE 2023:

Q. Find de Broglie wavelength of electron accelerated through 100 V.

Ans: $\lambda = \frac{12.27}{\sqrt{100}} = 1.227$ Å
