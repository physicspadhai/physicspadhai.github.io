Chapter 11: Dual Nature of Radiation and Matter ⚛️💡

1. Electron Emission

Metals se electron nikalne ke 4 tarike:

  • Thermionic: Heat se - CRT, vacuum tubes
  • Field Emission: Strong electric field se
  • Photoelectric: Light se - Photoelectric effect
  • Secondary: Fast electrons se bombard karne par

Work Function:

$$\phi_0 = h\nu_0$$ Minimum energy needed to eject electron from metal surface

2. Photoelectric Effect

Hertz 1887, Lenard 1900, Einstein 1905 explained. Light jab metal par padti hai to electron emit hote hain.

Experimental Observations:

  • $I_{photo} \propto$ Intensity of light
  • KE of photoelectrons independent of intensity
  • No time lag between light aur emission
  • Frequency > threshold frequency $\nu_0$ honi chahiye

Einstein's Photoelectric Equation:

$$h\nu = \phi_0 + KE_{max}$$ $$KE_{max} = eV_0 = h\nu - h\nu_0 = h(\nu - \nu_0)$$ $V_0$ = Stopping potential

Important Graphs:

  • I vs V: Saturation current $\propto$ intensity, Stopping potential same
  • KE_max vs ν: Straight line, slope = h, intercept = -φ₀
  • V₀ vs ν: Straight line, slope = h/e

PYQ 2024:

Q. Work function 2.14 eV. Light of 400nm falls. Find stopping potential.
Ans: $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda} = \frac{1240}{400} = 3.1$ eV
$eV_0 = E - \phi_0 = 3.1 - 2.14 = 0.96$ eV ⇒ $V_0 = 0.96$ V

🔥 Board Point: Wave theory explain nahi kar paayi: 1. No time lag 2. KE independent of intensity 3. Threshold frequency. Particle nature se ho gaya.

3. Photon

Light ka packet of energy. Rest mass zero.

Energy: $$E = h\nu = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$ Momentum: $$p = \frac{E}{c} = \frac{h\nu}{c} = \frac{h}{\lambda}$$ Mass: $$m = \frac{E}{c^2} = \frac{h\nu}{c^2}$$

4. Wave Nature of Matter - de Broglie

1924: Moving particle wave ki tarah behave karti hai.

$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$ For electron accelerated by V volts: $$\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2meV}} = \frac{1.227}{\sqrt{V}} \text{ nm}$$

de Broglie Wavelength for Particles:

For KE = K: $$\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2mK}}$$ For thermal neutrons: $$\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{3mkT}}$$

PYQ 2023:

Q. Find λ for electron accelerated through 100V.
Ans: $\lambda = \frac{1.227}{\sqrt{100}} = 0.1227$ nm = 1.227 Å

5. Davisson-Germer Experiment

1927: Electron diffraction se de Broglie hypothesis prove hua. Electron ki wave nature confirm.

Bragg's Law: $$2d\sin\theta = n\lambda$$ For Ni crystal: d = 0.91 Å, λ = 1.65 Å at 54V

6. Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle

Position aur momentum dono exact measure nahi kar sakte simultaneously.

$$\Delta x \cdot \Delta p \geq \frac{h}{4\pi}$$ $$\Delta E \cdot \Delta t \geq \frac{h}{4\pi}$$
