Chapter 8: Electromagnetic Waves 🌊📡

1. Displacement Current

Maxwell ne Ampere's law me correction kiya. Capacitor charging ke time conduction current nahi hota, par electric field change hota hai.

$$I_d = \varepsilon_0 \frac{d\phi_E}{dt}$$ Where $\phi_E$ = Electric flux

Maxwell-Ampere's Law:

$$\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0(I_c + I_d) = \mu_0 I_c + \mu_0\varepsilon_0\frac{d\phi_E}{dt}$$
🔥 Board Point: Conduction current + Displacement current = Continuous. Capacitor ke beech displacement current flow hota hai.

2. Maxwell's Equations

EM theory ki 4 basic equations:

  1. Gauss Law Electric: $\oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{q}{\varepsilon_0}$
  2. Gauss Law Magnetic: $\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A} = 0$ - Monopole exist nahi karte
  3. Faraday Law: $\oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l} = -\frac{d\phi_B}{dt}$
  4. Ampere-Maxwell Law: $\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 I_c + \mu_0\varepsilon_0\frac{d\phi_E}{dt}$

3. Electromagnetic Waves

Time varying electric field magnetic field produce karta hai, aur time varying magnetic field electric field produce karta hai. Ye dono ek doosre ko sustain karte hain aur wave form me propagate karte hain.

Properties of EM Waves:

  • Transverse nature: $\vec{E} \perp \vec{B} \perp$ direction of propagation
  • Self-sustaining: No medium required
  • Speed in vacuum: $c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\varepsilon_0}} = 3 \times 10^8$ m/s
  • Speed in medium: $v = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu\varepsilon}} = \frac{c}{n}$
  • $\vec{E}, \vec{B}$ aur propagation direction right hand rule follow karte hain
  • $E_0 = cB_0$ relation hota hai
$$c = \frac{E_0}{B_0} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\varepsilon_0}}$$

PYQ 2023:

Q. Write 4 characteristics of EM waves.
Ans: 1. Transverse 2. No medium needed 3. Speed = c 4. $\vec{E} \perp \vec{B} \perp \vec{k}$

4. Energy Density of EM Wave

$$u_E = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2 \text{ (Electric)}$$ $$u_B = \frac{1}{2\mu_0}B^2 \text{ (Magnetic)}$$ $$u_{avg} = u_E = u_B = \frac{1}{4}\varepsilon_0 E_0^2$$

Intensity:

$$I = u_{avg} \times c = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E_0^2 c$$

5. Momentum of EM Wave

EM waves momentum carry karti hain, isliye radiation pressure exert karti hain.

$$p = \frac{U}{c} \text{ for complete absorption}$$ $$p = \frac{2U}{c} \text{ for complete reflection}$$

6. Electromagnetic Spectrum

Wavelength ya frequency ke increasing order me arrangement:

Name Wavelength Range Frequency (Hz) Production Uses
Radio waves > 0.1 m 10⁴ - 10⁹ Accelerated charges in LC circuit Radio, TV, Mobile
Microwaves 0.1m - 1mm 10⁹ - 10¹¹ Klystron, Magnetron Radar, Microwave oven, Satellite
Infrared 1mm - 700nm 10¹¹ - 4×10¹⁴ Hot bodies, Vibrations Remote, Night vision, Physiotherapy
Visible Light 700nm - 400nm 4×10¹⁴ - 7×10¹⁴ Atomic transitions Vision, Photosynthesis
Ultraviolet 400nm - 1nm 10¹⁵ - 10¹⁷ Sun, Arc lamps Water purification, Forensics
X-rays 1nm - 10⁻³nm 10¹⁷ - 10²⁰ Fast electrons on metal Medical diagnosis, Crystallography
Gamma rays < 10⁻³ nm > 10²⁰ Nuclear reactions Cancer treatment, Sterilization

Important Points:

  • Gamma rays: Sabse jyada penetrating power
  • Microwave oven: Water molecules resonance se heat
