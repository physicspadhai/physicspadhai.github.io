Chapter 9: Ray Optics and Optical Instruments 🔍💡

1. Reflection of Light

Laws of Reflection:

  • Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection: $i = r$
  • Incident ray, reflected ray, normal - same plane me hote hain

Spherical Mirror Formula:

$$\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u}$$ $$f = \frac{R}{2}$$ New Cartesian Sign Convention use karo

Magnification:

$$m = \frac{h_i}{h_o} = -\frac{v}{u}$$ m -ve = Real inverted, m +ve = Virtual erect
🔥 Board Point: Concave mirror: Real + Virtual dono image. Convex mirror: Always Virtual, erect, diminished.

2. Refraction of Light

Snell's Law:

$$\frac{\sin i}{\sin r} = \frac{n_2}{n_1} = \frac{v_1}{v_2} = \frac{\lambda_1}{\lambda_2}$$ n = Refractive index = $\frac{c}{v}$

Real Depth vs Apparent Depth:

$$n = \frac{\text{Real Depth}}{\text{Apparent Depth}}$$

3. Total Internal Reflection (TIR)

Light denser se rarer medium me jaaye aur i > i_c ho to 100% reflection.

Critical Angle:

$$\sin i_c = \frac{n_{rarer}}{n_{denser}} = \frac{1}{n} \text{ if rarer = air}$$

Applications of TIR:

  • Mirage formation
  • Diamond sparkle - n=2.42, i_c=24.4°
  • Optical fibre communication
  • Prism: Erecting prism, 90° deviation

PYQ 2023:

Q. Define critical angle. Write conditions for TIR.
Ans: $i_c = \sin^{-1}(1/n)$. Conditions: 1. Denser to rarer 2. i > i_c

4. Refraction at Spherical Surface

$$\frac{n_2}{v} - \frac{n_1}{u} = \frac{n_2 - n_1}{R}$$

Lens Maker's Formula:

$$\frac{1}{f} = (n-1)\left(\frac{1}{R_1} - \frac{1}{R_2}\right)$$ For lens in air, $n = n_{lens}$

Thin Lens Formula:

$$\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u}$$ $$m = \frac{h_i}{h_o} = \frac{v}{u}$$

Power of Lens:

$$P = \frac{1}{f\text{(m)}} = \frac{100}{f\text{(cm)}}$$ Unit: Dioptre (D). Convex +ve, Concave -ve

5. Combination of Lenses

In Contact:

$$\frac{1}{F} = \frac{1}{f_1} + \frac{1}{f_2}$$ $$P = P_1 + P_2$$ $$m = m_1 \times m_2$$

6. Prism

$$i + e = A + \delta$$ $$r_1 + r_2 = A$$

Angle of Minimum Deviation:

$$n = \frac{\sin\left(\frac{A + \delta_m}{2}\right)}{\sin(A/2)}$$ At $\delta_m$, $i = e$, $r_1 = r_2 = A/2$

For Thin Prism:

$$\delta = (n-1)A$$

PYQ 2024:

Q. Derive prism formula for minimum deviation.
Ans: At $\delta_m$: i=e, r₁=r₂. Use $i+e=A+δ_m$ and $r₁+r₂=A$. Apply Snell's law.

7. Optical Instruments

1. Simple Microscope:

$$M = 1 + \frac{D}{f} \text{ (image at D)}$$ $$M = \frac{D}{f} \text{ (image at ∞)}$$ D = 25 cm least distance

2. Compound Microscope:

$$M = m_o \times M_e = \frac{v_o}{u_o}\left(1 + \frac{D}{f_e}\right)$$ $$L = v_o + u_e$$

3. Astronomical Telescope:

$$M = -\frac{f_o}{f_e} \text{ (normal adjustment)}$$ $$L = f_o + f_e$$ $$M = -\frac{f_o}{f_e}\left(1 + \frac{f_e}{D}\right) \text{ (final image at D)}$$

4. Reflecting Telescope (Cassegrain):

Concave mirror objective use karta hai. Advantages:

  • No chromatic aberration
  • No spherical aberration with parabolic mirror
  • High resolving power, large aperture possible
