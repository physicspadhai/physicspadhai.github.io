1. Rutherford's α-scattering

Most α-particles passed, few deflected, very few bounced back.

Conclusion: Atom has small +ve nucleus, electrons revolve around.

Impact Parameter:

$b = \frac{Ze^2 \cot(\theta/2)}{4\pi\epsilon_0 K}$

Distance of Closest Approach: $r_0 = \frac{2Ze^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0 K}$

2. Bohr Model Postulates

  • Electrons in stationary orbits: $mvr = \frac{nh}{2\pi}$
  • Energy radiated only when electron jumps: $h\nu = E_i - E_f$

3. Hydrogen Atom

Radius of nth orbit:

$r_n = 0.53 n^2$ Å = $n^2 a_0$

Velocity: $v_n = \frac{c}{137n} = 2.18×10^6 \frac{1}{n}$ m/s

Energy of nth orbit:

$E_n = -\frac{13.6}{n^2}$ eV

KE = $-E_n$, PE = $2E_n$, Total = $E_n$

4. Hydrogen Spectrum

$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R\left(\frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2}\right)$
R = 1.097×10⁷ m⁻¹

Previous Year Questions 2026

CBSE 2024:

Q. Energy of electron in H-atom is -3.4 eV. Find angular momentum.

Ans: $E_n = -13.6/n^2 = -3.4$ → $n = 2$. $L = nh/2\pi = 2h/2\pi = h/\pi$

CBSE 2023:

Q. Find shortest wavelength in Balmer series.

Ans: For shortest λ, $n_i = ∞$, $n_f = 2$. $\frac{1}{\lambda} = R(1/4 - 0)$ → $\lambda = 4/R = 3646$ Å
