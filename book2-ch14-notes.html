2. Intrinsic Semiconductor

Pure semiconductor. Si, Ge - 4 valence electrons. At T=0K insulator, T↑ conductivity↑

$$n_e = n_h = n_i$$ Conductivity: $$\sigma = e(n_e\mu_e + n_h\mu_h)$$ $\mu_e > \mu_h$ for Si, Ge

3. Extrinsic Semiconductor

Doping karke conductivity badhate hain.

n-type:

Pentavalent impurity - P, As, Sb. Donor level conduction band ke just neeche.

$$n_e >> n_h$$ Majority: electrons, Minority: holes

p-type:

Trivalent impurity - B, Al, Ga. Acceptor level valence band ke just upar.

$$n_h >> n_e$$ Majority: holes, Minority: electrons

Mass Action Law:

$$n_e n_h = n_i^2$$ Valid for both intrinsic & extrinsic
🔥 Board Point: Doping se conductivity 10⁶ times badh jaati hai. But net charge zero rehta hai.

4. p-n Junction Diode

p-type aur n-type ko join karne par depletion layer banti hai ~10⁻⁶m

Barrier Potential:

Si = 0.7V, Ge = 0.3V at room temp

Biasing:

Biasing Connection Depletion Width Resistance Current
Forward p to +ve, n to -ve Decreases Low ~10Ω mA order, diffusion
Reverse p to -ve, n to +ve Increases High ~10⁶Ω μA order, drift

I-V Characteristics:

$$I = I_s(e^{eV/kT} - 1)$$ I_s = Reverse saturation current

Knee Voltage:

Forward bias me current suddenly badhne lagta hai. Si=0.7V, Ge=0.3V

Breakdown:

  • Zener: Heavy doping, thin depletion, E field se electron pull. < 6V
  • Avalanche: Light doping, thick depletion, collision se ionisation. > 6V

PYQ 2024:

Q. Explain working of p-n junction in forward bias.
Ans: p to +ve, n to -ve. Barrier potential kam hota hai, depletion width ghat ti hai, majority carriers cross karte hain, current mA me.

5. Applications of Diode

1. Rectifier:

AC to DC conversion

  • Half Wave: Efficiency 40.6%, Ripple factor 1.21
  • Full Wave: Efficiency 81.2%, Ripple factor 0.48

2. Zener Diode:

Reverse bias me voltage regulator ke kaam aata hai.

$$V_{out} = V_Z = \text{constant}$$

3. Optoelectronic Devices:

  • LED: Forward bias, e-h recombination se light. GaAs, GaP
  • Photodiode: Reverse bias, light se current. Optical communication
  • Solar Cell: No bias, light se emf generate

6. Transistor

2 p-n junctions. 3 terminals: Emitter, Base, Collector. E heavily doped, B thin lightly doped, C moderately doped.

Types:

npn & pnp. Symbol me arrow emitter par, conventional current direction.

Working:

EB forward biased, CB reverse biased. Active region.

$$I_E = I_B + I_C$$ $I_B$ ≈ 2-5% of $I_E$, $I_C$ ≈ 95-98% of $I_E$

Current Gains:

$$\alpha = \frac{I_C}{I_E} < 1 \text{ (CB config)}$$ $$\beta = \frac{I_C}{I_B} >> 1 \text{ (CE config)}$$ $$\alpha = \frac{\beta}{1+\beta}, \quad \beta = \frac{\alpha}{1-\alpha}$$

Transistor as Amplifier (CE):

Current Gain: $$A_i = \beta$$ Voltage Gain: $$A_v = \beta \frac{R_C}{R_B}$$ Power Gain: $$A_p = \beta^2 \frac{R_C}{R_B}$$ Phase diff: 180° input-output
