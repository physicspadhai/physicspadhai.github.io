1. Composition & Size

Nucleus = Protons + Neutrons. $Z$ = atomic number, $A$ = mass number

Radius: $R = R_0 A^{1/3}$, where $R_0 = 1.2$ fm
Density = constant ≈ $2.3×10^{17}$ kg/m³

2. Mass Defect & Binding Energy

Mass Defect:

$\Delta m = [Zm_p + (A-Z)m_n] - M_{nucleus}$

Binding Energy:

$BE = \Delta m × c^2 = \Delta m × 931.5$ MeV

BE per nucleon max for Fe-56 ≈ 8.8 MeV. Curve peaks at A=56

3. Radioactivity

Spontaneous disintegration. Law:

$N = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$
Activity: $A = \lambda N = A_0 e^{-\lambda t}$

Half Life & Mean Life:

$T_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{\lambda}$
$\tau = \frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.44 T_{1/2}$

4. Nuclear Fission & Fusion

Fission: Heavy nucleus splits. Ex: $^{235}U + n → ^{141}Ba + ^{92}Kr + 3n + 200$ MeV

Fusion: Light nuclei combine. Ex: $4^1H → ^4He + 2e⁺ + 26.7$ MeV. Needs 10⁷ K temp

Previous Year Questions 2026

CBSE 2024:

Q. Define half life. A radioactive sample has activity 8000 dps after 4 half lives. Find initial activity.

Ans: $A = A_0/2^4$ → $8000 = A_0/16$ → $A_0 = 128000$ dps

CBSE 2023:

Q. Calculate BE per nucleon of $^{16}O$. Given $m_p=1.00727u$, $m_n=1.00866u$, $M_O=15.99491u$

Ans: $\Delta m = 8(1.00727+1.00866) - 15.99491 = 0.13253u$. BE = $0.13253×931.5 = 123.45$ MeV. BE/nucleon = $123.45/16 = 7.72$ MeV
