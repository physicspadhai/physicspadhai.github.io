Chapter 12: Atoms ⚛️

1. Alpha-Particle Scattering - Rutherford Experiment

1911: Geiger-Marsden ne gold foil par α-particles fire kiye.

Observations:

  • Most α-particles undeflected - Atom mostly empty space
  • Few deflected by small angles
  • 1 in 8000 deflected by > 90° - Nucleus exist karta hai

Impact Parameter:

$$b = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \frac{Ze^2\cot(\theta/2)}{K}$$ b = 0 for head-on collision, θ = 180°

Distance of Closest Approach:

$$r_0 = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \frac{2Ze^2}{K}$$ Size of nucleus ≈ $10^{-14}$ m
🔥 Board Point: Rutherford model fail: 1. Electron spiral karke nucleus me gir jaayega 2. Continuous spectrum explain nahi kar paaya

2. Bohr's Model of Hydrogen Atom

1913: Rutherford ki problems solve karne ke liye 3 postulates diye

Bohr's Postulates:

  1. Stationary Orbits: Electron bina energy lose kiye specific orbits me ghoomta hai. $mvr = \frac{nh}{2\pi}$
  2. Frequency Condition: $h\nu = E_i - E_f$ jab electron jump karta hai
  3. Angular Momentum Quantisation: $L = mvr = n\hbar$ where $\hbar = h/2\pi$

3. Bohr's Formulas for Hydrogen

Radius of nth Orbit:

$$r_n = \frac{\varepsilon_0 n^2 h^2}{\pi m e^2} = 0.53 \times n^2 \text{ Å}$$ $$r_n \propto n^2$$

Velocity of Electron:

$$v_n = \frac{e^2}{2\varepsilon_0 nh} = \frac{c}{137n} = 2.18 \times 10^6 \times \frac{1}{n} \text{ m/s}$$ $$v_n \propto \frac{1}{n}$$

Energy of Electron:

$$E_n = -\frac{me^4}{8\varepsilon_0^2 n^2 h^2} = -\frac{13.6}{n^2} \text{ eV}$$ $$E_n \propto \frac{1}{n^2}$$ KE = $-E_n$ = +13.6/n² eV, PE = 2E_n = -27.2/n² eV

Frequency & Time Period:

$$\nu_n = \frac{v_n}{2\pi r_n} \propto \frac{1}{n^3}$$ $$T_n = \frac{1}{\nu_n} \propto n^3$$

PYQ 2024:

Q. Find radius and energy of 2nd orbit of H-atom.
Ans: $r_2 = 0.53 \times 2^2 = 2.12$ Å
$E_2 = -13.6/2^2 = -3.4$ eV

4. Hydrogen Spectrum

Electron higher energy level se lower me jump kare to photon emit hota hai.

$$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R\left(\frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2}\right)$$ R = Rydberg constant = $1.097 \times 10^7$ m⁻¹

Spectral Series:

Series n_f n_i Region
Lyman 1 2,3,4... UV
Balmer 2 3,4,5... Visible
Paschen 3 4,5,6... IR
Brackett 4 5,6,7... IR
Pfund 5 6,7,8... IR

Series Limit:

Shortest wavelength = Maximum energy. $n_i = \infty$

$$\lambda_{min} = \frac{n_f^2}{R}$$ Lyman limit = 912 Å, Balmer limit = 3646 Å

PYQ 2023:

Q. Calculate wavelength of 2nd line of Balmer series.
Ans: Balmer: n_f=2, 2nd line: n_i=4
$\frac{1}{\lambda} = R\left(\frac{1}{2^2} - \frac{1}{4^2}\right) = R\left(\frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{16}\right) = \frac{3R}{16}$
$\lambda = \frac{16}{3R} = 4861$ Å

5. Energy Level Diagram

  • Ground State: n=1, E₁ = -13.6 eV
  • First Excited: n=2, E₂ = -3.4 eV
  • Ionization Energy: 13.6 eV for H-atom
  • Excitation Energy: E₂ - E₁ = 10.2 eV for first excited

6. Limitations of Bohr Model

  • Only for single electron atoms - H, He⁺, Li²⁺
  • Fine structure explain nahi kar paaya
  • Zeeman effect, Stark effect explain nahi hua
  • Heisenberg uncertainty violate karta hai
  • Intensity of spectral lines explain nahi hua
