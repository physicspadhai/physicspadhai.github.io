1. Displacement Current

Current due to changing electric field in capacitor.

$I_d = \epsilon_0 \frac{d\phi_E}{dt}$

Modified Ampere's Law:

$\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0(I_c + I_d) = \mu_0 I + \mu_0\epsilon_0\frac{d\phi_E}{dt}$

2. Electromagnetic Waves

Produced by accelerated charges. $\vec{E}$ ⊥ $\vec{B}$ ⊥ direction of propagation.

$c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\epsilon_0}} = 3 \times 10^8$ m/s
$c = \frac{E_0}{B_0}$

Properties: Transverse, no medium needed, carry energy & momentum.

CBSE 2024:

Q. Write 4 properties of electromagnetic waves. How is speed of EM wave in vacuum related to μ₀ and ε₀?

Ans: 1.Transverse 2.No medium 3.$c=3×10⁸$m/s 4.$\vec{E}⊥\vec{B}⊥\vec{k}$. $c = 1/\sqrt{\mu_0\epsilon_0}$

CBSE 2023:

Q. What is displacement current? Why was it needed?

Ans: $I_d = \epsilon_0 d\phi_E/dt$. Needed to make Ampere's law consistent for charging capacitor where conduction current is discontinuous.
