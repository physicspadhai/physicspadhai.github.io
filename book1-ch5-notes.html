1. Magnetic Flux

Number of magnetic field lines crossing a surface.

$\phi_B = \vec{B} \cdot \vec{A} = BA\cos\theta$
SI Unit: Weber (Wb)
1 Wb = 1 Tm²

2. Faraday's Laws

1st Law: EMF induced when flux linked changes.

2nd Law: Magnitude of induced EMF ∝ Rate of change of flux.

$e = -\frac{d\phi_B}{dt}$
For N turns: $e = -N\frac{d\phi_B}{dt}$

-ve sign = Lenz's Law

3. Lenz's Law

Direction of induced current opposes the cause producing it.

Conservation: Lenz law follows Law of Conservation of Energy.

4. Motional EMF

EMF induced in conductor moving in magnetic field:

$e = Blv$
B ⊥ l ⊥ v

5. Self & Mutual Induction

Self Induction:

$e = -L\frac{dI}{dt}$
L = Self Inductance, Unit: Henry (H)

Mutual Induction:

$e_2 = -M\frac{dI_1}{dt}$
M = Mutual Inductance

6. AC Generator

Principle: Electromagnetic Induction

$e = NBA\omega \sin\omega t$
$e_0 = NBA\omega$ = Peak EMF

Previous Year Questions 2026

CBSE 2024:

Q. State Lenz's law. Show that it follows law of conservation of energy.

Ans: Induced current opposes change. If it supported, flux would increase → more current → more flux. Infinite energy, violates COE. Hence opposes.

CBSE 2023:

Q. A coil of 100 turns, area 0.1 m² rotates in B = 0.2 T at 50 rps. Find max induced EMF.

Ans: $e_0 = NBA\omega = 100 \times 0.2 \times 0.1 \times 2\pi \times 50 = 628$ V
