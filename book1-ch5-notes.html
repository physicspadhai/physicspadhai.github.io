Chapter 5: Magnetism and Matter 🧲

1. Bar Magnet

Equivalent to solenoid. Has North pole and South pole. Monopole doesn't exist.

Magnetic Dipole Moment:

$$\vec{m} = q_m(2\vec{l})$$ Direction: S to N pole. Unit: A-m² or J/T

Magnetic Field on Axial Line:

$$B_{axial} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\frac{2m}{r^3} \text{ for } r >> l$$

Magnetic Field on Equatorial Line:

$$B_{equatorial} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\frac{m}{r^3}$$
🔥 Board Point: $B_{axial} = 2 \times B_{equatorial}$ at same distance. Electric dipole ke formulas se compare karo.

2. Torque on Magnetic Dipole in Uniform B

$$\vec{\tau} = \vec{m} \times \vec{B}$$ $$\tau = mB\sin\theta$$

Potential Energy:

$$U = -\vec{m} \cdot \vec{B} = -mB\cos\theta$$ Stable equilibrium: $\theta = 0°$, U = -mB minimum
Unstable equilibrium: $\theta = 180°$, U = +mB maximum

3. Gauss's Law in Magnetism

$$\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A} = 0$$

Meaning: Net magnetic flux through closed surface is always zero. Magnetic monopoles don't exist. Magnetic field lines form closed loops.

4. Earth's Magnetism

Elements of Earth's Magnetic Field:

  • Declination (D): Angle between geographic meridian and magnetic meridian
  • Dip/Inclination (I): Angle made by Earth's total field $B_E$ with horizontal
  • Horizontal Component: $B_H = B_E \cos I$
  • Vertical Component: $B_V = B_E \sin I$
$$B_E = \sqrt{B_H^2 + B_V^2}$$ $$\tan I = \frac{B_V}{B_H}$$

PYQ 2023:

Q. Define magnetic declination and dip. At a place $B_H = B_V$. Find angle of dip.
Ans: $\tan I = B_V/B_H = 1 \Rightarrow I = 45°$

5. Magnetic Properties of Materials

1. Magnetisation (M):

$$\vec{M} = \frac{\vec{m}_{net}}{V}$$ Magnetic moment per unit volume. Unit: A/m

2. Magnetic Intensity (H):

$$\vec{H} = \frac{\vec{B}}{\mu_0} - \vec{M}$$ Unit: A/m

3. Magnetic Susceptibility (χ):

$$\chi_m = \frac{M}{H}$$ Dimensionless. Measures how easily material gets magnetised.

4. Relative Permeability (μᵣ):

$$\mu_r = 1 + \chi_m = \frac{\mu}{\mu_0} = \frac{B}{B_0}$$

Relation between B, H, M:

$$\vec{B} = \mu_0(\vec{H} + \vec{M}) = \mu_0\mu_r\vec{H} = \mu\vec{H}$$

Curie Law:

$$\chi_m = \frac{C}{T}$$ C = Curie constant

Curie-Weiss Law:

$$\chi_m = \frac{C}{T - T_C}$$ T_C = Curie temperature

PYQ 2024:

Q. Distinguish between diamagnetic and ferromagnetic materials.
Ans: Dia: χ -ve, μᵣ<1, weakly repelled. Ferro: χ large +ve, μᵣ>>1, strongly attracted, retains magnetism.

7. Hysteresis Loop

For ferromagnetic materials: B lags behind H. Area of loop = Energy loss per cycle.

  • Retentivity: Value of B when H = 0. Remaining magnetism.
  • Coercivity: Value of H needed to make B = 0. Demagnetising field.

Soft Iron vs Steel:

Property Soft Iron Steel
Retentivity High High
Coercivity Low High
Hysteresis Loss Less More
Use Electromagnet, Transformer core Permanent magnet

8. Permanent Magnets & Electromagnets

Permanent Magnet:

High retentivity, High coercivity. Ex: Steel, Alnico

Electromagnet:

High μᵣ, Low retentivity, Low coercivity. Ex: Soft iron
