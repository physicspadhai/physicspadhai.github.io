Chapter 1: Electric Charges and Fields ⚡

1. Electric Charge

  • Definition: Intrinsic property of matter responsible for electric forces.
  • Types: Positive charge and Negative charge.
  • SI Unit: Coulomb (C)
  • Quantization: $q = \pm ne$, where $n = 1,2,3...$ and $e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}$ C
  • Conservation: Total charge of an isolated system remains constant.
🔥 Board Point: Like charges repel, unlike charges attract. Charge is additive and invariant.

2. Coulomb's Law

The force between two point charges is directly proportional to the product of charges and inversely proportional to the square of distance between them.

$$F = k\frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$$ Where $k = 9 \times 10^9$ N m²/C², $\varepsilon_0 = 8.854 \times 10^{-12}$ C²/N m²

Vector Form:

$$\vec{F}_{12} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2}\hat{r}_{12}$$

PYQ 2023:

Q. Two point charges +9e and +e are at 16 cm apart. At what point between them should a third charge q be placed so that it is in equilibrium?
Ans: 12 cm from +9e charge. Use $F_1 = F_2$

3. Electric Field

Region around a charge where its electrostatic force can be felt by another charge.

$$\vec{E} = \frac{\vec{F}}{q_0} \quad \text{or} \quad \vec{E} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{q}{r^2}\hat{r}$$ SI Unit: N/C or V/m

Electric Field Lines - Properties:

  • Start from +ve charge, end on -ve charge
  • Never intersect each other
  • Tangent gives direction of E at that point
  • Relative density indicates field strength

4. Electric Dipole

System of two equal and opposite charges separated by small distance 2a.

Dipole Moment:

$$\vec{p} = q(2\vec{a})$$ Direction: From -q to +q. Unit: C-m

Field on Axial Line:

$$E_{axial} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{2pr}{(r^2-a^2)^2} \approx \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{2p}{r^3} \text{ for } r >> a$$

Field on Equatorial Line:

$$E_{equatorial} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{p}{(r^2+a^2)^{3/2}} \approx \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{p}{r^3} \text{ for } r >> a$$

Torque on Dipole in Uniform E:

$$\vec{\tau} = \vec{p} \times \vec{E} \quad \text{or} \quad \tau = pE\sin\theta$$

5. Electric Flux

Total number of electric field lines passing normally through a surface.

$$\phi_E = \vec{E} \cdot \vec{A} = EA\cos\theta$$ Unit: N m²/C or V-m

6. Gauss's Law

Total electric flux through a closed surface is $\frac{1}{\varepsilon_0}$ times the net charge enclosed.

$$\oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{q_{enclosed}}{\varepsilon_0}$$

Applications:

1. Infinite Line Charge: $E = \frac{\lambda}{2\pi\varepsilon_0 r}$

2. Infinite Plane Sheet: $E = \frac{\sigma}{2\varepsilon_0}$

3. Spherical Shell: Outside $E = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{q}{r^2}$, Inside E = 0

PYQ 2024:

Q. State Gauss's law. Derive expression for electric field due to uniformly charged infinite plane sheet.
Ans: Use Gaussian cylinder. Flux through curved surface = 0. $E = \sigma/2\varepsilon_0$
