Ch 1: Electric Charges and Fields

1. Electric Charge

Definition: Intrinsic property of matter which causes electric force.

Properties:

  • Additive: Total charge = algebraic sum
  • Quantized: $q = \pm ne$, where $e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} C$
  • Conserved: Total charge of isolated system is constant
SI Unit: Coulomb (C)
1 Coulomb = charge on $6.25 \times 10^{18}$ electrons

2. Coulomb's Law

Force between two point charges:

$F = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2}$
$\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} = 9 \times 10^9 Nm^2/C^2$

Vector form: $\vec{F}_{12} = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} \hat{r}_{12}$

3. Electric Field

Force per unit positive test charge.

$\vec{E} = \frac{\vec{F}}{q_0}$ or $\vec{E} = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q}{r^2} \hat{r}$
SI Unit: N/C or V/m

Electric Field Lines:

  • Start from +ve, end on -ve charge
  • Never intersect
  • Density ∝ Field strength

4. Electric Dipole

Two equal & opposite charges separated by small distance.

Dipole moment: $\vec{p} = q \times 2\vec{a}$
Direction: -ve to +ve charge
Unit: Cm

Field on Axial Line:

$E_{axial} = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{2p}{r^3}$ for r >> a

Field on Equatorial Line:

$E_{equi} = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{p}{r^3}$ for r >> a

5. Gauss's Law

Total flux through closed surface = 1/ε₀ times charge enclosed.

$\oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{q_{enc}}{\epsilon_0}$

Applications:

  • Infinite Line Charge: $E = \frac{\lambda}{2\pi\epsilon_0 r}$
  • Infinite Sheet: $E = \frac{\sigma}{2\epsilon_0}$
  • Spherical Shell: Outside $E = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q}{r^2}$, Inside E = 0

Previous Year Questions 2026

CBSE 2024:

Q. An electric dipole of dipole moment $4 \times 10^{-9}$ Cm is placed in uniform electric field of $5 \times 10^4$ N/C. Find torque when dipole makes 30° with field.

Ans: $\tau = pE \sin\theta = 4 \times 10^{-9} \times 5 \times 10^4 \times \sin30° = 10^{-4}$ Nm

CBSE 2023:

Q. State Gauss's law. Using it, derive expression for electric field due to uniformly charged infinite plane sheet.

Ans: Gauss Law: $\oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = q/\epsilon_0$. For sheet, take cylindrical Gaussian surface. Flux = $2EA = \sigma A / \epsilon_0$ → $E = \sigma/2\epsilon_0$

CBSE 2022:

Q. Two point charges +q and -q are separated by distance 2a. Find electric field at point on axial line at distance r from center.

Ans: $E = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{2pr}{(r^2-a^2)^2}$ where $p = q \times 2a$
