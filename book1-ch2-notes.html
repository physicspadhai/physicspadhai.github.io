Chapter 2: Electrostatic Potential and Capacitance 🔋

1. Electric Potential

Work done per unit positive charge in bringing it from infinity to that point against electrostatic force.

$$V = \frac{W}{q_0} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{q}{r}$$ SI Unit: Volt (V) = J/C. Scalar quantity.

Potential Difference:

$$V_B - V_A = \frac{W_{AB}}{q_0} = -\int_A^B \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l}$$
🔥 Board Point: Potential is zero at infinity. Electric field is negative gradient of potential: $\vec{E} = -\vec{\nabla}V$

2. Potential due to Point Charge

$$V = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{q}{r}$$

Potential due to Electric Dipole:

Axial: $V = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{p\cos\theta}{r^2}$

Equatorial: $V = 0$ because $\theta = 90°$

3. Equipotential Surface

  • Surface where potential is same at every point
  • Work done in moving charge on it is zero
  • Electric field is always perpendicular to equipotential surface
  • Two equipotential surfaces never intersect

4. Potential Energy

System of Two Charges:

$$U = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{q_1 q_2}{r}$$

System of Three Charges:

$$U = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\left[\frac{q_1 q_2}{r_{12}} + \frac{q_2 q_3}{r_{23}} + \frac{q_1 q_3}{r_{13}}\right]$$

5. Potential Energy of Dipole in External Field

$$U = -\vec{p} \cdot \vec{E} = -pE\cos\theta$$ Stable equilibrium: $\theta = 0°$, Unstable: $\theta = 180°$

PYQ 2023:

Q. Derive expression for potential energy of electric dipole in uniform electric field.
Ans: $U = -pE\cos\theta$. Minimum at $\theta = 0°$

6. Capacitance

Ability of conductor to store charge. $C = \frac{Q}{V}$

SI Unit: Farad (F) = C/V. 1 F = 1 C/V

Parallel Plate Capacitor:

$$C = \frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d}$$ With dielectric: $C = K\frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d} = KC_0$

7. Combination of Capacitors

Series:

$$\frac{1}{C_s} = \frac{1}{C_1} + \frac{1}{C_2} + \frac{1}{C_3}$$ Same charge, potential divides: $V = V_1 + V_2 + V_3$

Parallel:

$$C_p = C_1 + C_2 + C_3$$ Same potential, charge divides: $Q = Q_1 + Q_2 + Q_3$

8. Energy Stored in Capacitor

$$U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{1}{2}QV = \frac{Q^2}{2C}$$

Energy Density:

$$u = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2$$ Unit: J/m³

PYQ 2024:

Q. Three capacitors 2μF, 3μF, 6μF are connected in series. Find equivalent capacitance.
Ans: $\frac{1}{C} = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{6} = 1$, So C = 1 μF

9. Dielectrics

Insulating materials which transmit electric effect without conduction.

  • Polar: Have permanent dipole moment. Ex: H₂O, NH₃
  • Non-polar: No permanent dipole. Ex: H₂, N₂, CO₂

Effect of Dielectric:

When dielectric slab of thickness t is inserted: $C = \frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d - t + t/K}$
