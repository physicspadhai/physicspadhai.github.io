1. Electric Current

Rate of flow of charge through a conductor.

$I = \frac{q}{t}$
SI Unit: Ampere (A)
1 A = 1 C/s

Current Density: $J = \frac{I}{A}$ Unit: A/m²

2. Ohm's Law

At constant temp, current through conductor ∝ potential difference.

$V = IR$
R = Resistance, Unit: Ohm (Ω)

Resistance depends on:

$R = \rho \frac{l}{A}$
ρ = Resistivity, Unit: Ωm

3. Kirchhoff's Laws

KCL - Junction Rule:

Sum of currents entering = Sum of currents leaving

$\sum I = 0$ at a junction

KVL - Loop Rule:

Algebraic sum of potential drops in closed loop = 0

$\sum V = 0$ in a closed loop

4. Wheatstone Bridge

Bridge is balanced when:

$\frac{P}{Q} = \frac{R}{S}$
Then $V_G = 0$, $I_G = 0$

5. Cells in Series & Parallel

Series:

$E_{eq} = E_1 + E_2 + ...$
$r_{eq} = r_1 + r_2 + ...$

Parallel - Identical cells:

$E_{eq} = E$
$r_{eq} = r/n$

Previous Year Questions 2026

CBSE 2024:

Q. Using Kirchhoff's rules, calculate current in 40Ω resistor in the given network.

Ans: Apply KVL in loops. On solving, $I_{40Ω} = 0.2$ A

CBSE 2023:

Q. Define drift velocity. Derive relation $I = neAv_d$.

Ans: $v_d$ = Average velocity of electrons opposite to field. In time dt, charge crossing A = $neAv_d dt$ → $I = q/dt = neAv_d$
