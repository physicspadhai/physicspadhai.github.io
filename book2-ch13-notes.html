Chapter 13: Nuclei ⚛️💥

1. Composition of Nucleus

Nucleus me protons + neutrons hote hain. Collectively nucleons kehte hain.

$_Z^A X$ : Z = Atomic number = Protons, A = Mass number = p + n
N = Neutrons = A - Z

Terms:

  • Isotopes: Same Z, different A. Ex: $_1^1H$, $_1^2H$, $_1^3H$
  • Isobars: Same A, different Z. Ex: $_1^3H$, $_2^3He$
  • Isotones: Same N, different Z. Ex: $_1^3H$, $_2^4He$ N=2

2. Size of Nucleus

$$R = R_0 A^{1/3}$$ $R_0 = 1.2 \times 10^{-15}$ m = 1.2 fm
Nuclear density constant ≈ $2.3 \times 10^{17}$ kg/m³
🔥 Board Point: Nucleus ka size atom se $10^5$ times chota, par mass 99.9% yahi hota hai.

3. Mass-Energy & Nuclear Binding Energy

Einstein's Mass-Energy:

$$E = mc^2$$ 1 amu = 931.5 MeV

Mass Defect:

$$\Delta m = [Zm_p + Nm_n] - M_{nucleus}$$

Binding Energy:

$$BE = \Delta m \times c^2 = \Delta m \times 931.5 \text{ MeV}$$ Binding Energy per nucleon: $$BE/A$$

BE/A Curve:

  • Max BE/A ≈ 8.8 MeV for Fe-56 - Most stable
  • Light nuclei fuse karte hain - Fusion
  • Heavy nuclei fission karte hain - Fission
  • BE/A kam for very light A<20 and very heavy A>170

PYQ 2024:

Q. Mass of $_8^{16}O$ = 15.9949 u. Find BE/nucleon.
Ans: Z=8, N=8. Mass defect = (8×1.00727 + 8×1.00866) - 15.9949 = 0.12754 u
BE = 0.12754 × 931.5 = 118.8 MeV
BE/A = 118.8/16 = 7.42 MeV/nucleon

4. Nuclear Force

Strongest force in nature. Properties:

  • Charge independent: p-p, n-n, p-n same
  • Short range: ~ 2-3 fm tak hi
  • Saturated: Ek nucleon limited nucleons se hi interact
  • Spin dependent
  • Non-central force

5. Radioactivity

Henry Becquerel 1896. Unstable nuclei spontaneously decay karte hain.

Types of Decay:

Type Emission Change Example
α-decay $_2^4He$ Z-2, A-4 $_ {92}^{238}U \to _ {90}^{234}Th + α$
β⁻ decay e⁻ + ν̄ Z+1, A same $_ {6}^{14}C \to _ {7}^{14}N + e⁻ + ν̄$
β⁺ decay e⁺ + ν Z-1, A same $_ {11}^{22}Na \to _ {10}^{22}Ne + e⁺ + ν$
γ-decay γ-photon Z, A same Excited nucleus → Ground + γ

6. Law of Radioactive Decay

$$\frac{dN}{dt} = -\lambda N$$ $$N = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$$ $\lambda$ = Decay constant

Half Life:

$$T_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{\lambda}$$ Time in which half nuclei decay

Mean Life:

$$\tau = \frac{1}{\lambda} = 1.44 T_{1/2}$$

Activity:

$$R = \lambda N = R_0 e^{-\lambda t}$$ Unit: Becquerel (Bq) = 1 decay/s
1 Curie = $3.7 \times 10^{10}$ Bq

PYQ 2023:

Q. Half life 2.5 days. After 10 days what fraction remains?
Ans: n = 10/2.5 = 4 half lives
Fraction = $\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^4 = \frac{1}{16}$

7. Nuclear Fission

Heavy nucleus 2 lighter nuclei me tootta hai. Energy release hoti hai.

$_ {92}^{235}U + _0^1n \to _ {56}^{141}Ba + _ {36}^{92}Kr + 3_0^1n + 200$ MeV

Chain Reaction:

Neutrons aur fission cause karte hain. K = Multiplication factor

  • K < 1: Subcritical - reaction stops
  • K = 1: Critical - steady - Nuclear reactor
  • K > 1: Supercritical - Atom bomb

Nuclear Reactor:

  • Fuel: U-235, Pu-239
  • Moderator: Heavy water, Graphite - neutron slow karta hai
  • Control Rods: Cd, B - neutron absorb
  • Coolant: Water, liquid Na - heat remove

8. Nuclear Fusion

Light nuclei mil kar heavy nucleus banate hain. Sun ki energy ka source.

$_1^2H + _1^2H \to _1^3H + _1^1H + 4.03$ MeV
$_1^2H + _1^3H \to _2^4He + _0^1n + 17.6$ MeV

Condition: ~10⁸ K temperature chahiye - Thermonuclear reaction
