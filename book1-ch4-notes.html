Chapter 4: Moving Charges and Magnetism 🧲

1. Magnetic Field - Oersted's Experiment

Current carrying conductor produces magnetic field around it. Magnetic field is produced by moving charges only.

🔥 Board Point: Stationary charge → only electric field. Moving charge → both electric + magnetic field.

2. Biot-Savart Law

Magnetic field due to current element $I d\vec{l}$ at point P:

$$d\vec{B} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I d\vec{l} \times \vec{r}}{r^3}$$ $$|dB| = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I dl \sin\theta}{r^2}$$ Where $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7}$ Tm/A

Direction:

Given by Right Hand Thumb Rule. Curl fingers in direction of current, thumb gives B direction.

3. Magnetic Field due to Current Carrying Conductor

1. Straight Wire - Finite Length:

$$B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{4\pi r}(\sin\phi_1 + \sin\phi_2)$$

2. Infinite Straight Wire:

$$B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$$

3. At Centre of Circular Coil:

$$B = \frac{\mu_0 N I}{2R}$$ N = number of turns, R = radius

4. On Axis of Circular Coil:

$$B = \frac{\mu_0 N I R^2}{2(R^2 + x^2)^{3/2}}$$ At centre x=0, gives above formula.

PYQ 2023:

Q. Derive expression for magnetic field at centre of circular current loop.
Ans: Use Biot-Savart law. Integrate $dl = Rd\theta$ from 0 to 2π. $B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2R}$

4. Ampere's Circuital Law

Line integral of magnetic field over closed loop = $\mu_0$ times total current threading the loop.

$$\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 I_{enclosed}$$

Applications:

1. Infinite Wire: $B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$ - Same as Biot-Savart

2. Solenoid: Inside $B = \mu_0 n I$, Outside B ≈ 0. n = turns per unit length

3. Toroid: Inside $B = \frac{\mu_0 N I}{2\pi r}$, Outside B = 0

5. Force on Moving Charge - Lorentz Force

$$\vec{F} = q(\vec{E} + \vec{v} \times \vec{B})$$ Magnetic force: $\vec{F}_m = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$ or $F = qvB\sin\theta$

Direction:

Fleming's Left Hand Rule. For +ve charge. For -ve charge, reverse direction.

🔥 Board Point: Magnetic force does NO work because $\vec{F} \perp \vec{v}$. It only changes direction, not speed/KE.

6. Motion in Magnetic Field

Case 1: $\vec{v} \parallel \vec{B}$ or $\vec{v}$ anti-parallel

$\theta = 0°$ or $180°$, F = 0. Charge moves in straight line.

Case 2: $\vec{v} \perp \vec{B}$

Charge moves in circular path.

$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r} \Rightarrow r = \frac{mv}{qB} = \frac{p}{qB}$$ $$T = \frac{2\pi m}{qB}, \quad f = \frac{qB}{2\pi m}$$

Case 3: $\vec{v}$ at angle θ

Helical path. Pitch = $v_\parallel T = v\cos\theta \times \frac{2\pi m}{qB}$

7. Cyclotron

Device to accelerate charged particles using crossed E and B fields.

$$\text{Cyclotron frequency: } f = \frac{qB}{2\pi m}$$ $$\text{Max KE: } KE_{max} = \frac{q^2 B^2 R^2}{2m}$$

Limitations:

  • Can't accelerate electrons - relativistic mass increase
  • Can't accelerate neutrons - no charge

8. Force on Current Carrying Conductor

$$\vec{F} = I(\vec{L} \times \vec{B})$$ $$F = BIL\sin\theta$$

9. Force Between Two Parallel Currents

$$\frac{F}{l} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$$ Parallel currents attract, Anti-parallel repel.

Definition of 1 Ampere:

If I₁ = I₂ = 1A, r = 1m, then F/l = 2 × 10⁻⁷ N/m

10. Torque on Current Loop

$$\vec{\tau} = \vec{M} \times \vec{B} = NIAB\sin\theta$$ Where $M = NIA$ = Magnetic dipole moment

11. Moving Coil Galvanometer

Current measuring device. Principle: $\tau_{deflecting} = \tau_{restoring}$

$$NIAB = k\phi \Rightarrow I = \frac{k}{NAB}\phi = G\phi$$ G = Galvanometer constant

Conversion to Ammeter:

Connect low resistance shunt S in parallel: $S = \frac{I_g R_g}{I - I_g}$

Conversion to Voltmeter:

Connect high resistance R in series: $R = \frac{V}{I_g} - R_g$

Device Resistance Connection
Ammeter Very Low Series
Voltmeter Very High Parallel
Galvanometer Medium -

PYQ 2024:

Q. How to convert galvanometer to ammeter? Derive formula for shunt.
Ans: Connect shunt S parallel. $I_g R_g = (I-I_g)S \Rightarrow S = \frac{I_g R_g}{I-I_g}$
