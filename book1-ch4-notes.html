1. Bar Magnet

Magnetic dipole moment:

$\vec{M} = m \times 2\vec{l}$
m = Pole strength, 2l = Magnetic length
Unit: Am²

Field on Axial Line:

$B_{axial} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{2M}{r^3}$ for r >> l

Field on Equatorial Line:

$B_{equi} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{M}{r^3}$ for r >> l

2. Earth's Magnetism

Elements of Earth's Field:

  • Declination (D): Angle b/w geographic & magnetic meridian
  • Dip/Inclination (I): Angle b/w total field & horizontal
  • Horizontal Component: $B_H = B \cos I$
$\tan I = \frac{B_V}{B_H}$
$B = \sqrt{B_H^2 + B_V^2}$

3. Magnetic Materials

PropertyDiamagneticParamagneticFerromagnetic
Susceptibility χ-ve & small+ve & small+ve & large
Permeability μᵣμᵣ < 1μᵣ > 1μᵣ >> 1
ExampleCu, Bi, H₂OAl, Ca, O₂Fe, Ni, Co

4. Hysteresis Loop

  • Retentivity: Magnetism left when H = 0
  • Coercivity: Reverse H needed to make B = 0
  • Soft Iron: Low retentivity, low coercivity → Electromagnets
  • Steel: High retentivity, high coercivity → Permanent magnets

Previous Year Questions 2026

CBSE 2024:

Q. Define magnetic susceptibility. How does it vary for dia, para and ferromagnetic materials?

Ans: χ = I/H. Dia: χ = -ve small ~10⁻⁵. Para: χ = +ve small ~10⁻⁵. Ferro: χ = +ve large ~10² to 10⁵

CBSE 2023:

Q. A bar magnet of dipole moment 2 Am² is placed in magnetic field of 0.5 T. Find torque when angle is 30°.

Ans: $\tau = MB\sin\theta = 2 \times 0.5 \times \sin30° = 0.5$ Nm
