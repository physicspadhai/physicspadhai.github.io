1. Elements of Communication System

Block Diagram: Information → Transmitter → Channel → Receiver → User

  • Transducer: Converts one form to electrical. Ex: Mic, Photo-detector
  • Noise: Unwanted signals. Reduces quality of transmission
  • Attenuation: Loss of signal strength with distance
  • Bandwidth: Range of frequencies over which signal transmitted

2. Modulation - Why Needed?

  • Audio freq 20Hz-20kHz → Antenna size huge: $\lambda = c/f$ ≈ 15km for 20kHz
  • Low freq can't travel long distance
  • Mixing of signals prevented

Modulation: Low freq message signal superimposed on high freq carrier wave

3. Amplitude Modulation AM

Amplitude of carrier varied as per message signal.

$c(t) = A_c \sin(\omega_c t)$
$m(t) = A_m \sin(\omega_m t)$
Modulated: $c_m(t) = A_c[1 + \mu \sin(\omega_m t)]\sin(\omega_c t)$

Modulation Index: $\mu = A_m/A_c$. For no distortion, $0 < \mu ≤ 1$

Frequencies: $f_c$, $f_c + f_m$ USB, $f_c - f_m$ LSB

Bandwidth of AM: $2f_m$

Power: $P_t = P_c\left(1 + \frac{\mu^2}{2}\right)$

4. Frequency Modulation FM

Frequency of carrier varied as per message signal. Amplitude constant.

Advantages: Noise free, High efficiency

Disadvantages: Large bandwidth needed

6. Satellite Communication

Geostationary: Height 36000 km, Time period 24 hr, Same direction as earth

Uplink: 5.925-6.425 GHz, Downlink: 3.7-4.2 GHz

7. Optical Fiber Communication

Works on Total Internal Reflection. Core + Cladding

Advantages: High bandwidth, Low loss, No interference, Light weight

Previous Year Questions 2026

CBSE 2024:

Q. A carrier wave of 1.5 MHz is amplitude modulated by 10 kHz signal. Find bandwidth and frequencies present.

Ans: BW = $2f_m = 2×10 = 20$ kHz. Frequencies: 1500 kHz, 1510 kHz USB, 1490 kHz LSB

CBSE 2023:

Q. Why is FM preferred over AM for music transmission?

Ans: FM has better noise immunity, higher fidelity, constant amplitude. AM affected by electrical noise.

CBSE 2022:

Q. What is modulation index? Why should it be ≤ 1 for AM?

Ans: $\mu = A_m/A_c$. If $\mu > 1$, overmodulation occurs → envelope distortion → information loss
